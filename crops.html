                                              Onion

                                              An essential vegetable with good storage life.

                                              Planting: May-June
                                              Harvest: Oct-Nov
                                              Spacing: 30cm x 10cm
                                              Pest: Watch for thrips and onion flies
                                              Storage: Store in a dry, ventilated place

                                              Overview

                                              Onion is an essential vegetable with good storage life and market demand. It is used in almost all dishes and is a key ingredient in many cuisines. Onions can be stored for several months, making them a reliable source of income.

                                              Best Planting Season

                                              May to June with the rainy season. Onions can also be planted year-round with irrigation.

                                              Soil Type

                                              Well-drained loamy or sandy-loam soils with pH 6.0-7.0. Onions prefer fertile, well-aerated soils.

                                              Land Preparation

                                              Plough to a depth of 20-25 cm. Harrow to create a fine seedbed. Add organic matter for better yields.

                                              Seed Rate

                                              3-5 kg per acre for direct planting. Use high-quality certified seeds.

                                              Water Requirement

                                              400-600 mm per growing season. Onions need consistent moisture, especially during bulb formation.

                                              Common Pests

                                              • Thrips - Apply insecticides and maintain good plant nutrition.
                                              • Onion Flies - Use resistant varieties and practice crop rotation.
                                              • Nematodes - Use nematode-resistant varieties and practice rotation.

                                              Common Diseases

                                              • Downy Mildew - Apply fungicides and ensure proper spacing.
                                              • Purple Blotch - Use disease-free seeds and practice crop rotation.
                                              • Fusarium Wilt - Use resistant varieties and ensure good drainage.

                                              Market Tips

                                              Onions have a stable market throughout the year. Dry and cure properly for good storage. There is high demand for onions in urban areas and for processing. Link with food processing companies and export markets.

                                              Pumpkin

                                              A hardy crop that provides food and income.

                                              Planting: May-June
                                              Harvest: Oct-Nov
                                              Spacing: 150cm x 100cm
                                              Pest: Watch for fruit flies and powdery mildew
                                              Storage: Store in a cool, dry place

                                              Overview

                                              Pumpkin is a hardy crop that provides food and income. It is rich in vitamins and minerals, especially vitamin A. Pumpkins can be eaten fresh, processed into puree, or used in traditional dishes. The leaves and flowers are also edible.

                                              Best Planting Season

                                              May to June with the rainy season. Pumpkins can also be planted year-round in some areas.

                                              Soil Type

                                              Well-drained loamy or sandy-loam soils with pH 6.0-7.5. Pumpkins prefer fertile, well-aerated soils.

                                              Land Preparation

                                              Plough to a depth of 20-25 cm. Create mounds or ridges for planting. Add organic manure for better yields.

                                              Seed Rate

                                              2-3 kg per acre. Plant 2-3 seeds per mound at a spacing of 150cm x 100cm.

                                              Water Requirement

                                              400-600 mm per growing season. Pumpkins need adequate moisture during fruiting.

                                              Common Pests

                                              • Fruit Flies - Use traps and apply appropriate pesticides.
                                              • Powdery Mildew - Apply fungicides and ensure proper spacing.
                                              • Leaf Beetles - Use insecticides and practice crop rotation.

                                              Common Diseases

                                              • Powdery Mildew - Apply fungicides and maintain good plant spacing.
                                              • Downy Mildew - Use resistant varieties and ensure good air circulation.
                                              • Anthracnose - Use disease-free seeds and practice crop rotation.

                                              Market Tips

                                              Pumpkins have a ready market in local communities and urban areas. Process into puree or value-added products for better prices. There is growing demand for pumpkin seeds as a healthy snack. Link with food processing companies.

                                              🌧 May – June

                                              Yam

                                              A valuable root crop with high market demand.

                                              Planting: May-June
                                              Harvest: Oct-Nov
                                              Spacing: 100cm x 100cm
                                              Pest: Watch for yam beetles and nematodes
                                              Storage: Store in a cool, dark, well-ventilated place

                                              Overview

                                              Yam is a valuable root crop with high market demand. It is a major staple in many parts of South Sudan and is used in various dishes. Yam can be stored for several months, providing food security and income.

                                              Best Planting Season

                                              May to June with the rainy season. Yams need a long growing season of 8-10 months.

                                              Soil Type

                                              Well-drained loamy or sandy-loam soils with pH 5.5-6.5. Yams prefer fertile soils with good organic matter.

                                              Land Preparation

                                              Plough to a depth of 25-30 cm. Create mounds or ridges for planting. Add organic manure for better yields.

                                              Seed Rate

                                              Plant 1,000-1,500 seed yams per acre. Use healthy, disease-free seed yams.

                                              Water Requirement

                                              900-1200 mm per growing season. Yams need consistent moisture for good tuber development.

                                              Common Pests

                                              • Yam Beetles - Use resistant varieties and apply appropriate pesticides.
                                              • Nematodes - Use nematode-resistant varieties and practice crop rotation.
                                              • Mealybugs - Use biological control and apply insecticides.

                                              Common Diseases

                                              • Yam Mosaic Virus - Use virus-free planting materials.
                                              • Anthracnose - Apply fungicides and ensure proper spacing.
                                              • Root Rot - Ensure good drainage and use disease-free seeds.

                                              Market Tips

                                              Yams have a strong market demand. Store properly to sell during the off-season for better prices. There is growing demand for yams in urban areas and for processing. Link with food processing companies and export markets.

                                                                          Mangoes

                                                                          Sweet and juicy fruits that are a favorite across the country.

                                                                          Planting: March-April
                                                                          Harvest: Nov-Jan
                                                                          Spacing: 1000cm x 800cm
                                                                          Pest: Watch for fruit flies and mealybugs
                                                                          Storage: Store at room temperature until ripe

                                                                          Overview

                                                                          Mangoes are sweet and juicy fruits that are a favorite across South Sudan. They are rich in vitamins and provide income for many farmers. Mangoes can be consumed fresh, juiced, or processed.

                                                                          Best Planting Season

                                                                          March to April at the beginning of the rainy season. Mangoes are also planted in other months with irrigation.

                                                                          Soil Type

                                                                          Well-drained loamy or sandy-loam soils with pH 5.5-7.0. Mangoes prefer fertile, well-aerated soils.

                                                                          Land Preparation

                                                                          Plough to a depth of 30-40 cm. Create planting holes. Add organic manure for better yields.

                                                                          Seed Rate

                                                                          Plant 50-100 grafted seedlings per acre. Use high-quality, disease-free seedlings.

                                                                          Water Requirement

                                                                          800-1200 mm per growing season. Mangoes need consistent moisture during flowering and fruiting.

                                                                          Common Pests

                                                                          • Fruit Flies - Use traps and apply appropriate pesticides.
                                                                          • Mealybugs - Use biological control and apply insecticides.
                                                                          • Aphids - Use insecticides or biological control methods.

                                                                          Common Diseases

                                                                          • Anthracnose - Apply fungicides and maintain good plant spacing.
                                                                          • Powdery Mildew - Apply fungicides and ensure proper air circulation.
                                                                          • Mango Malformation - Use resistant varieties and practice proper pruning.

                                                                          Market Tips

                                                                          Mangoes have strong demand in local and export markets. Grade and package properly for better prices. There is growing demand for processed mango products like juice, jam, and dried mango. Link with food processing companies and export agents.

                                                                          🌧 Green Belt Region

                                                                          Coffee

                                                                          A valuable cash crop that grows well in the Green Belt with abundant rainfall and moderate temperatures.

                                                                          Planting: Beginning of rains
                                                                          Harvest: Dry season
                                                                          Spacing: 250cm x 250cm
                                                                          Pest: Watch for coffee berry borer
                                                                          Storage: Dry and store in cool, dry place

                                                                          Overview

                                                                          Coffee is a valuable cash crop that grows well in the Green Belt with abundant rainfall and moderate temperatures. It is an important source of income for many farmers in South Sudan.

                                                                          Best Planting Season

                                                                          Beginning of the rainy season. Coffee is a perennial crop that requires careful management.

                                                                          Soil Type

                                                                          Deep, well-drained volcanic or loamy soils with pH 5.5-6.5. Coffee prefers rich, organic soils with good drainage.

                                                                          Land Preparation

                                                                          Plough to a depth of 30-40 cm. Create planting holes. Add organic manure and lime if needed.

                                                                          Seed Rate

                                                                          Plant 1,000-2,000 seedlings per acre. Use high-quality, disease-free seedlings.

                                                                          Water Requirement

                                                                          1200-1800 mm per growing season. Coffee needs consistent moisture and humidity.

                                                                          Common Pests

                                                                          • Coffee Berry Borer - Use traps and apply appropriate pesticides.
                                                                          • Leaf Rust - Apply fungicides and use resistant varieties.
                                                                          • Nematodes - Use nematode-resistant varieties and practice rotation.

                                                                          Common Diseases

                                                                          • Coffee Leaf Rust - Use resistant varieties and apply fungicides.
                                                                          • Coffee Berry Disease - Use resistant varieties and practice proper field hygiene.
                                                                          • Coffee Wilt - Use disease-free planting materials and practice crop rotation.

                                                                          Market Tips

                                                                          Coffee has excellent export potential. There is strong demand for high-quality coffee in international markets. Process and roast for better prices. Link with coffee buyers and exporters. Join coffee cooperatives for better bargaining power.

                                                                          🌧 Green Belt Region

                                                                          Tea

                                                                          An important cash crop introduced in the 1970s, grown in the Green Belt with abundant rainfall and moderate temperatures.

                                                                          Planting: Rainy season
                                                                          Harvest: Year-round
                                                                          Spacing: 100cm x 75cm
                                                                          Pest: Watch for tea mosquito bug
                                                                          Storage: Process and dry soon after harvest

                                                                          Overview

                                                                          Tea is an important cash crop introduced in the 1970s, grown in the Green Belt with abundant rainfall and moderate temperatures. It provides income for many farmers in South Sudan.

                                                                          Best Planting Season

                                                                          Rainy season. Tea is a perennial crop that requires careful management and regular harvesting.

                                                                          Soil Type

                                                                          Deep, well-drained acidic soils with pH 4.5-5.5. Tea prefers soils rich in organic matter.

                                                                          Land Preparation

                                                                          Plough to a depth of 30-40 cm. Create planting rows. Add organic manure and improve drainage.

                                                                          Seed Rate

                                                                          Plant 1,000-1,200 seedlings per acre. Use high-quality, disease-free seedlings.

                                                                          Water Requirement

                                                                          1200-1800 mm per growing season. Tea needs consistent moisture throughout the year.

                                                                          Common Pests

                                                                          • Tea Mosquito Bug - Use biological control and apply appropriate pesticides.
                                                                          • Tea Mites - Use miticides and maintain good plant nutrition.
                                                                          • Nematodes - Use nematode-resistant varieties and practice rotation.

                                                                          Common Diseases

                                                                          • Blister Blight - Apply fungicides and ensure good air circulation.
                                                                          • Brown Blight - Use resistant varieties and practice proper pruning.
                                                                          • Root Rot - Ensure good drainage and use disease-free planting materials.

                                                                          Market Tips

                                                                          Tea has a strong market for local consumption and export. Process and package properly for better prices. There is growing demand for high-quality tea in international markets. Link with tea processors and exporters.

                                                                          🌧 Green Belt Region

                                                                          Tobacco

                                                                          A cash crop grown in the Green Belt region, particularly in Yei, Kajo-Keji, Maridi, and Magwi.

                                                                          Planting: Beginning of rains
                                                                          Harvest: Dry season
                                                                          Spacing: 90cm x 60cm
                                                                          Pest: Watch for aphids and hornworms
                                                                          Storage: Cure and dry before storage

                                                                          Overview

                                                                          Tobacco is a cash crop grown in the Green Belt region, particularly in Yei, Kajo-Keji, Maridi, and Magwi. It provides income for many farmers and has both local and export market potential.

                                                                          Best Planting Season

                                                                          Beginning of the rainy season. Tobacco requires careful management throughout the growing season.

                                                                          Soil Type

                                                                          Well-drained sandy or loamy soils with pH 5.5-6.5. Tobacco prefers fertile, well-aerated soils.

                                                                          Land Preparation

                                                                          Plough to a depth of 20-25 cm. Harrow to create a fine seedbed. Ensure good drainage and weed control.

                                                                          Seed Rate

                                                                          200-300 grams per acre. Use high-quality, disease-free seeds.

                                                                          Water Requirement

                                                                          400-600 mm per growing season. Tobacco needs consistent moisture but avoid waterlogging.

                                                                          Common Pests

                                                                          • Aphids - Use insecticides or biological control methods.
                                                                          • Hornworms - Apply appropriate pesticides and use biological control.
                                                                          • Budworms - Use resistant varieties and practice crop rotation.

                                                                          Common Diseases

                                                                          • Tobacco Mosaic Virus - Use virus-free planting materials and control pests.
                                                                          • Leaf Spot - Apply fungicides and ensure proper spacing.
                                                                          • Fusarium Wilt - Use resistant varieties and practice crop rotation.

                                                                          Market Tips

                                                                          Tobacco has both local and export market potential. Cure and process properly for better prices. Link with tobacco buyers and processing companies. Join farmer groups for collective marketing and better prices.
