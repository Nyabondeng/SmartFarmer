Resilient Crops

Understanding Climate Change in South Sudan

Climate change is making farming harder in South Sudan. Rains are becoming more unpredictable. Droughts and floods are more common. Farmers need to adapt to survive.

In the past, farmers knew when to plant based on the seasons. Now, the seasons are changing. Some years the rain comes late. Other years it stops early. Farmers who adapt to these changes will have better harvests.

Farmer's Tip:

Pay attention to weather patterns in your area. Talk to older farmers about what they have seen.

Drought-Resistant Crops

Some crops can survive dry conditions better than others. Plant these when rain is uncertain:

  • ✓ Sorghum: Very drought-resistant, grows well in dry areas
  • ✓ Millet: Another hardy grain that tolerates drought
  • ✓ Cassava: Can survive dry periods and poor soils
  • ✓ Groundnuts: Tolerate dry conditions and improve soil
Farmer's Tip:

If you expect less rain, plant more sorghum and millet. They are reliable even in hard times.

Rainwater Harvesting

Collecting rainwater can help you cope with dry spells.

  • ✓ Build simple water collection systems
  • ✓ Store water in tanks or barrels
  • ✓ Use stored water for irrigation during dry periods
  • ✓ Dig ponds to collect runoff water
Farmer's Tip:

Even a small tank can make a big difference during a dry spell.

Early Planting

Planting early can help your crops get enough water before the rains stop.

  • ✓ Plant as soon as the first rains arrive
  • ✓ Use short-maturing varieties that finish before the dry season
  • ✓ Prepare your land early so you are ready when rain comes

Weather Forecasts

Knowing the weather helps you plan your farming.

  • ✓ Listen to local weather reports on the radio
  • ✓ Use the Smart Farmer USSD service for updates (*131#)
  • ✓ Watch for signs in nature (birds, insects, clouds)
  • ✓ Share weather information with other farmers
Farmer's Tip:

Older farmers know a lot about weather patterns. Learn from them.

Summary

  • ✓ Climate change is making farming harder in South Sudan
  • ✓ Plant drought-resistant crops like sorghum, millet, and cassava
  • ✓ Harvest rainwater and plant early
  • ✓ Listen to weather forecasts
