Understanding Market Prices

Knowing market prices helps you get a fair price for your crops. Prices change based on supply and demand.

  • ✓ Check local market prices before selling
  • ✓ Prices are usually higher right before harvest and lower after harvest
  • ✓ Talk to other farmers about prices in different markets
  • ✓ Use the Smart Farmer USSD service (*131#) for market updates
Farmer's Tip:

If prices are low, consider storing your crops and selling later.

When to Sell

Timing matters. Selling at the right time can increase your income by up to 30%.

  • ✓ Sell when prices are high
  • ✓ Avoid selling right after harvest when prices are low
  • ✓ Store crops to sell during the lean season (when prices rise)
  • ✓ Watch market trends and plan ahead
Farmer's Tip:

Many farmers sell immediately after harvest. If you can wait a few months, you will get much better prices.

Group Selling

Working with other farmers gives you more power in the market.

  • ✓ Sell in groups with other farmers
  • ✓ Bulk selling gives you more bargaining power
  • ✓ Share transport costs with other farmers
  • ✓ Form or join a farmers' cooperative
Farmer's Tip:

A group of farmers can get better prices than a single farmer. Strength in numbers.

Grading Your Produce

Better quality means better prices.

  • ✓ Sort your produce by size and quality
  • ✓ Remove damaged or diseased crops
  • ✓ Clean and package your produce well
  • ✓ Better quality gets a higher price
Farmer's Tip:

Buyers will pay more for clean, well-sorted produce. Take time to prepare your crops properly.

Keeping Records

Keeping simple records helps you make better decisions.

  • ✓ Write down what you sold, how much, and for what price
  • ✓ Track your expenses (seed, fertilizer, transport)
  • ✓ Compare your profits from season to season
  • ✓ Use the Smart Farmer Crop Log to track your harvests

Summary

  • ✓ Check market prices before selling
  • ✓ Sell when prices are high
  • ✓ Sell in groups with other farmers
  • ✓ Grade your produce for better prices
  • ✓ Keep records of your sales and expenses
