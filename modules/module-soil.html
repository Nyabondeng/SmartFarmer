Years of Soil Health

Why Soil Health Matters

Soil is the foundation of farming. Healthy soil produces healthy crops. In South Sudan, many farmers face declining soil fertility because of over-farming and poor practices.

When soil is healthy, it holds water, provides nutrients, and supports strong root growth. When soil is poor, crops struggle to grow and yields are low.

Farmer's Tip:

Good soil is like a bank account. You need to put back what you take out. Add organic matter every season.

Improving Soil Fertility

There are many ways to improve your soil naturally:

  • ✓ Compost: Add decomposed organic matter to the soil
  • ✓ Animal manure: Use cow, goat, or chicken manure
  • ✓ Green manure: Plant cover crops and plough them in
  • ✓ Crop rotation: Plant different crops each season
  • ✓ Intercropping: Plant nitrogen-fixing crops like groundnuts with other crops
Farmer's Tip:

Groundnuts are a great crop for improving soil fertility. They add nitrogen to the soil.

Crop Rotation

Planting the same crop in the same field every season depletes the soil. Crop rotation helps break pest cycles and keep soil healthy.

  • ✓ Year 1: Sorghum or Maize
  • ✓ Year 2: Groundnuts (adds nitrogen)
  • ✓ Year 3: Millet
  • ✓ Year 4: Cassava
  • ✓ Repeat the cycle
Farmer's Tip:

Rotate crops to break pest cycles and keep the soil from becoming exhausted.

Mulching

Mulch is a layer of organic material placed on top of the soil. It helps conserve water, reduce weeds, and improve soil health.

  • ✓ Use dry grass, leaves, or crop residues
  • ✓ Apply a layer 5-10cm thick
  • ✓ Do not let mulch touch the plant stems
  • ✓ Replace mulch as it breaks down
Farmer's Tip:

Mulch keeps the soil cool and moist. It is especially important in the dry season.

Avoiding Over-Cultivation

Over-cultivation damages soil structure and reduces fertility.

  • ✓ Do not plough too often or too deep
  • ✓ Keep the soil covered with mulch or cover crops
  • ✓ Use minimum tillage techniques
  • ✓ Avoid working wet soil

Summary

  • ✓ Add compost or manure regularly
  • ✓ Rotate crops every season
  • ✓ Use mulch to conserve moisture
  • ✓ Avoid over-cultivation
  • ✓ Plant nitrogen-fixing crops like groundnuts
