Post-Harvest Handling

Reduce crop losses after harvest with proper drying, storage, and handling techniques.

📉 Why Post-Harvest Matters

In South Sudan, up to 20% of crops are lost after harvest. This means less food for your family and less money from selling. Proper post-harvest handling can reduce these losses significantly.

Post-harvest losses happen when crops are not dried properly, stored poorly, or handled roughly. Insects, rodents, and mold are the main causes of these losses.

Farmer's Tip:

Every crop you save is food you don't have to buy. Good storage is good farming.

Harvesting at the Right Time

Harvesting at the right time is the first step to reducing losses.

  • ✓ Sorghum: Harvest when grains are hard and dry (September-October)
  • ✓ Maize: Harvest when leaves around the cob turn brown (August-September)
  • ✓ Millet: Harvest when grains turn golden brown (September-October)
  • ✓ Groundnuts: Harvest when pods are mature (October-November)
  • ✓ Cassava: Harvest 8-12 months after planting
Farmer's Tip:

If you harvest too early, grains are soft and will not store well. If you harvest too late, grains may fall off the plant.

Drying Crops Properly

Drying is the most important step in post-harvest handling. Moisture causes rot and attracts insects.

  • ✓ Dry crops on a clean surface, not on the ground
  • ✓ Turn crops regularly to dry evenly
  • ✓ Cover crops at night to protect from dew
  • ✓ Crops are ready when they break when bent, not bend
  • ✓ Test for dryness: bite a grain. If it cracks, it is ready
Farmer's Tip:

Never store crops that are not completely dry. Moisture will ruin everything.

Storage Methods

Good storage keeps your crops safe from pests and weather.

  • ✓ Use clean, dry containers
  • ✓ Make sure containers are rodent-proof
  • ✓ Add ash or crushed neem leaves to keep insects away
  • ✓ Store in a cool, dry place
  • ✓ Check your stored crops every 2 weeks
Farmer's Tip:

Ash is free and works well to keep insects out of stored grain. Mix it with the grain before storing.

Selling Your Surplus

If you have more than you need, selling can bring extra income.

  • ✓ Sell when market prices are good
  • ✓ Grade your produce (sort by size and quality)
  • ✓ Sell in groups with other farmers for better prices
  • ✓ Keep records of your sales
  • ✓ Join a farmers' cooperative if available
Farmer's Tip:

Don't sell everything at once. Store some crops to sell when prices are higher.

Summary

  • ✓ Harvest at the right time
  • ✓ Dry crops completely before storing
  • ✓ Store in clean, dry containers
  • ✓ Use ash to keep insects away
  • ✓ Sell surplus when prices are good
