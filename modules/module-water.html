Water Saving Methods

Rainwater Collection

Collecting rainwater is one of the simplest ways to get water for your crops. In South Sudan, the rainy season can bring a lot of water. If you capture it, you can use it later when the rains stop.

  • ✓ Use barrels or tanks to collect rainwater from roofs
  • ✓ Dig small ponds or trenches to capture runoff
  • ✓ Cover water containers to prevent evaporation
  • ✓ Use collected water for vegetable gardens and small crops
Farmer's Tip:

Every drop counts. Even a small barrel can water a vegetable garden for days.

When to Water

Watering at the right time saves water and helps your crops grow better.

  • ✓ Water early in the morning (before 9am)
  • ✓ Or water late in the evening (after 5pm)
  • ✓ Avoid watering during the middle of the day
  • ✓ Water the base of the plant, not the leaves
  • ✓ Water deeply rather than frequently
Farmer's Tip:

Watering early in the morning reduces evaporation. More water gets to the roots.

Irrigation Methods

Simple irrigation methods can help your crops survive dry spells:

  • ✓ Bucket irrigation: Carry water in buckets and pour at the base of each plant
  • ✓ Drip irrigation: Use a small pipe or tube with holes to deliver water slowly to the roots
  • ✓ Furrow irrigation: Dig small channels between rows and fill them with water
  • ✓ Watering cans: Use a watering can for small vegetable gardens
Farmer's Tip:

Drip irrigation uses less water than other methods. Even a simple bottle with small holes can work.

Avoiding Waterlogging

Too much water can be just as bad as too little.

  • ✓ Make sure your fields have good drainage
  • ✓ Do not plant in areas where water pools
  • ✓ Create raised beds to keep roots above water
  • ✓ If water is pooling, dig small channels to drain it
Farmer's Tip:

Waterlogged soil can kill roots. Cassava, especially, does not like wet feet.

Mulching for Water Conservation

Mulch helps keep moisture in the soil. It is one of the best ways to save water.

  • ✓ Apply a 5-10cm layer of organic material
  • ✓ Use dry grass, leaves, or crop residues
  • ✓ Mulch reduces evaporation from the soil surface
  • ✓ It also keeps the soil cool and reduces weeds
Farmer's Tip:

Mulch is a free way to save water. It is one of the cheapest and best things you can do for your farm.

Summary

  • ✓ Collect rainwater in barrels or tanks
  • ✓ Water early in the morning or late in the evening
  • ✓ Use simple irrigation methods like bucket or drip
  • ✓ Avoid waterlogging
  • ✓ Apply mulch to conserve moisture
