Introduction to Planting

Planting is the most important step in farming. If you plant correctly, your crops will grow strong and produce a good harvest. In South Sudan, the rainy season is the best time to plant.

Many farmers in South Sudan rely on rain-fed agriculture. This means you plant when the rains come and harvest when the crops are ready. Knowing the right planting techniques can make the difference between a good harvest and a poor one.

When to Plant

The best time to plant in South Sudan is May to June, at the beginning of the rainy season. This gives your crops enough water to grow strong roots.

  • ✓ Plant Sorghum in May-June
  • ✓ Plant Maize in May-June
  • ✓ Plant Millet in May-June
  • ✓ Plant Groundnuts in May-June
  • ✓ Plant Cassava in March-April
Farmer's Tip:

Do not plant too early or too late. If you plant too early, the seeds may rot in wet soil. If you plant too late, the rains may stop before your crops are ready.

How to Plant

Follow these steps for good planting:

  • ✓ Prepare the soil: Plough to a depth of 20cm to loosen the soil.
  • ✓ Choose the right seeds: Use clean, disease-free seeds.
  • ✓ Plant at the right depth: 2-5cm depending on the crop.
  • ✓ Space crops properly: Avoid overcrowding.
  • ✓ Water after planting: If possible, water the field after planting.
Farmer's Tip:

Plant seeds on the same day you prepare the soil. This helps keep moisture in the soil.

Spacing Guide

Proper spacing ensures your crops have enough room to grow. Overcrowding leads to weak plants and poor harvests.

  • ✓ Sorghum: 75cm x 25cm
  • ✓ Maize: 75cm x 50cm
  • ✓ Millet: 60cm x 20cm
  • ✓ Groundnuts: 50cm x 15cm
  • ✓ Cassava: 100cm x 100cm

Weed Control

Weeds compete with your crops for water, nutrients, and sunlight. Remove weeds within the first 3 weeks after planting.

  • ✓ Remove weeds by hand or with a hoe.
  • ✓ Weed during the morning or evening when it is cool.
  • ✓ Keep the field clean throughout the growing season.
Farmer's Tip:

Do not let weeds grow too tall. Tall weeds can shade your crops and reduce their growth.

Summary

Key takeaways:

  • ✓ Plant at the start of the rainy season (May-June)
  • ✓ Use clean, disease-free seeds
  • ✓ Space crops properly
  • ✓ Plant at the right depth (2-5cm)
  • ✓ Weed within the first 3 weeks
