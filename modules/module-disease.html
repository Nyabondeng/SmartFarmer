Spot and stop crop diseases early to protect your harvest.

Common Diseases

Common Crop Diseases

Knowing the signs of disease helps you act quickly.

  • ✓ Cassava Mosaic Disease: Yellow and distorted leaves on cassava
  • ✓ Leaf Spot: Brown or yellow spots on leaves
  • ✓ Rust: Orange or brown powder on leaves
  • ✓ Wilting: Plants droop and die suddenly
  • ✓ Rot: Soft, decaying areas on stems or fruits
Farmer's Tip:

Check your fields weekly. Early detection is key to controlling disease.

How to Spot Disease

Look for these signs of disease in your crops:

  • ✓ Spots on leaves (brown, yellow, or black)
  • ✓ Wilting or drooping plants
  • ✓ Rotting stems or fruits
  • ✓ Leaves turning yellow or falling off
  • ✓ White or powdery growth on leaves
Farmer's Tip:

Look under leaves and at the base of plants. Diseases often start in hidden places.

Prevention

Preventing disease is better than curing it:

  • ✓ Use disease-resistant seed varieties
  • ✓ Rotate crops every season
  • ✓ Keep fields clean and free of weeds
  • ✓ Space plants properly for air circulation
  • ✓ Water the soil, not the leaves
Farmer's Tip:

Healthy plants resist disease better. Feed your soil and water regularly.

What to Do When Disease Appears

Act fast when you see signs of disease:

  • ✓ Remove and burn diseased plants immediately
  • ✓ Do not compost diseased plants
  • ✓ Avoid walking through wet fields
  • ✓ Use disease-free seeds next season
  • ✓ Ask your extension officer for help
Farmer's Tip:

Burning diseased plants is the best way to stop disease from spreading.

Clean Tools and Fields

Disease can spread through dirty tools and fields.

  • ✓ Clean tools after working in diseased fields
  • ✓ Use clean water and soap
  • ✓ Let tools dry in the sun
  • ✓ Remove crop residue after harvest

Summary

  • ✓ Know the signs of common diseases
  • ✓ Inspect your fields weekly
  • ✓ Prevent disease with resistant varieties and crop rotation
  • ✓ Remove and burn diseased plants
  • ✓ Keep tools and fields clean
